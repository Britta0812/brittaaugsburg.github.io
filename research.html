Research

Research Areas

My research focuses on three domains: Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH), early childhood development (ECD), and nutrition. My work on WASH and ECD is primarily based in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia, using large-scale randomised controlled trials combined with novel data collection such as lab-in-the-field experiments; my research on nutrition focuses on the UK, analysing large-scale secondary data as part of the NIHR-funded Healthy Weight Policy Research Unit. A central theme across my work is understanding how interventions can be designed and delivered effectively at scale, in close collaboration with governments and implementing partners.

Filter by area

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)

I study WASH through large-scale field experiments in India, Pakistan, and Nigeria, examining constraints to adoption and sustained use as well as impacts on child health and broader outcomes. I co-founded the annual WASH Economics conference and contribute to the field through editorial and synthesis work, including a special issue in the Journal of Development Economics and in World Development, and a VoxDev literature review.

Early Childhood Development (ECD)

I study early childhood development with a focus on early-life environments, including parental stimulation, early learning, and health systems. I have conducted randomised controlled trials in India and lead large-scale evaluations in Ghana in collaboration with the Ministries of Education and Health, covering parental engagement, kindergarten quality, and maternal and newborn outcomes.

Nutrition

My work on nutrition focuses on the UK context. I use large-scale secondary data to analyse dietary behaviour and food environments. I am a Co-lead of the NIHR-funded Healthy Weight Policy Research Unit, which generates evidence to inform policies aimed at improving population diet and reducing obesity for the Department of Health and Social Care.

Other

I began my research career studying microfinance in India, Mongolia, and Bosnia and Herzegovina. This work shaped my research trajectory, from early data collection and grant funding to my first publications, and led to subsequent work on sanitation through the study of microloans for household investment. I have also done work on COVID-19, studying debunking of misinformation and vaccine acceptance and hesitancy.
